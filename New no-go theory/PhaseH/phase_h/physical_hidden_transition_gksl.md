## 1. Claim boundary: why this does not consume RISEI

This calculation is deliberately restricted to the scalar, single-sector SMRT
object

\[
R_{S,\Gamma}=\chi_{\mathrm{full},\Gamma}
-\chi^{(S)}_{\mathrm{cut},\Gamma}.
\]

The probe source, sector intervention, functional, and readout are fixed for
the whole calculation. The readout is always the single coherence
\(p=e_{\rho_{10}}\). No observable rotation, comparison of \(N_\infty\) and
\(N_1\), minimal irreducible sector order \(k_{\min}\), or multi-sector
composition is used.

Accordingly:

- **SMRT owns:** the Class-III-to-Class-II transition of one fixed scalar
  full-cut response, \(\nu[R_S]:0\to1\), while the total response stays
  protected.
- **RISEI retains:** observable-dependent protection depth, Pattern (b),
  functional hierarchy, and irreducible signals generated by joint sector
  interventions.

The Phase-H result therefore supplies the missing physical foundation for
SMRT without replacing the RISEI flagship claim.

## 2. Physical model

Hilbert-space basis:

\[
\{|0\rangle,|1\rangle,|2\rangle,|3\rangle\}.
\]

- \(|0\rangle\): ground state.
- \(|1\rangle,|2\rangle\): metastable states retained in the protected block.
- \(|3\rangle\): fast state with \(\Gamma\)-scaled decay.

All dissipation is implemented by non-negative Lindblad jumps to the ground:

\[
L_j=\sqrt{\gamma_j}|0\rangle\langle j|\quad(j=1,2,3),
\qquad
L_{3,\Gamma}=\sqrt{\Gamma d_3}|0\rangle\langle3|.
\]

The weak two-tone probe is fixed. To first order, the optical coherences

\[
x=(\rho_{10},\rho_{20},\rho_{30})^T
\]

obey

\[
(\Gamma D+B)x=c,
\qquad
D=\operatorname{diag}(0,0,1/2).
\]

The full model contains a Hermitian coupling
\(J_{12}|1\rangle\langle2|+\mathrm{h.c.}\). The sector cut sets only
\(J_{12}=0\), while keeping the source, readout, detunings, and every
dissipator unchanged.

\[
\boxed{
J_{12}^{\star}=\left(-i\,a\,\frac{c_2}{c_1}\right)^*

\[

## 5. Interpretation

This is the physical GKSL version of the SMRT flagship phenomenon:

\[
\boxed{
\nu[\chi_{\mathrm{full}}]=0
\quad\text{while}\quad
\nu[R_S]:0\longrightarrow1.
}
\]

The measured full response remains finite and changes smoothly. What changes
is the asymptotic order of the response attributable to the selected sector.
The transition is therefore invisible to classification based only on the
total spectrum.

The zero has codimension two in the physical amplitude-phase control plane.
Adding the detuning \(\Delta_2\) produces a one-dimensional cancellation line;
the transverse Jacobian remains full rank. This separates the result from a
trivial zero caused by switching off the sector.
